Abstract

Tornadoes are among the most destructive weather phenomena and yet not fully understood. Enhancing knowledge of such events will lead to improved prediction and damage mitigation protocols. In conjunction with advances in computer vision, the structure of tornadoes can be better understood through 3D modeling. In this work, we propose a semi-automated framework for generating 3D models of tornadoes, specifically, using advances inverse differentiable rendering and novel view synthesis (NVS). We demonstrate one can effective reconstruct a tornado using an array of cameras. We validate this pipeline in the lab using a tornado machine.

Method Overview

Gaussian Splatting models scenes as collections of 3D Gaussians that are rendered using a real-time rasterization pipeline. Our approach enables fast training and high-quality rendering. Please refer to our paper and GitHub repository for more details.
